About FC Barcelona

  • Little bit of Braça.

    FC Barcelona, in full Fútbol Club Barcelona, also called Barça, Spanish professional football (soccer) club located in Barcelona. FC Barcelona is renowned for its historically skillful and attractive brand of attacking football that places an emphasis on flowing, open play. The team is part of a wider sports and social club with thousands of members.

  • History of Barça

    FC Barcelona was formed in 1899 by businessman Joan Gamper, who advertised for players in a local Barcelona sports magazine. The club’s first trophy was the Copa Macaya (Catalan championship) in 1902, and in 1910 “Barça” won the Copa del Rey (“King’s Cup”)—Spain’s leading national football cup competition—for the first time. In total, Barcelona has won 30 Copas del Rey, more than any other team.

Lionel Andrés Messi

Messi <Rosario, Argentina, 24 June 1987> is the greatest player of all time and FC Barcelona record setter. Trained at La Masia, he will always be associated with Barça as the greatest player ever to wear the blaugrana shirt.

Leo Messi's footballing career started in 1995 at Newell's Old Boys, where he played until the year 2000. At the age of 13, Lionel Messi crossed the Atlantic to try his luck in Barcelona, and joined the Under 14s. Messi made spectacular progress at each of the different age levels, climbing through the ranks to Barça C, followed by Barça B and the first team in record time. .

2009, A MAGICAL YEAR

Under the direction of Josep Guardiola, the team had a dream 12 months, winning every trophy possible. Six titles that they claimed with authority, Barça on the field were untouchable. At the end of the season there were celebrations and the trophies kept coming for the rest of the year.

LUIS ENRIQUE: ARRIVAL AND A TREBLE

Gerardo Martino took over for Tito Vilanova in the summer of 2013 but, despite a good start, the left after the 2013/14 season having only won the Spanish Super Cup. Luis Enrique, a beloved former Barça player, took Martino’s place as coach in the summer of 2014 and success immediately followed. In his first year as coach, Luis Enrique would attain the second treble in Barça history, just six seasons after the first. FC Barcelona won the League, Copa del Rey and Champions League in one magical season. The same team, consisting of top players like Messi, Iniesta, Neymar Jr and Luis Suárez went on to win a total of five titles in 2015, adding the UEFA Super Cup and the Club World Cup to the treble. To that treble the team would add four more trophies the following season: the European Super Cup, the Club World Cup, the league and the Copa del Rey. Furthermore, in the 2016/17 season, the team won the Copa del Rey, their third in a row.

Camp Nou

has hosted the Club's first team football matches since September 1957. The new Barça stadium replaced the old Les Corts ground, which, despite having a capacity for 48,000 spectators (after several extensions), became too small due to the great expansion of the Barça fans at the end of the 1940s . The architects Francesc Mitjans Miró and Josep Soteras Mauri (with the collaboration of Lorenzo García Barbón) designed the Stadium, which was built between 1955 and 1957 using concrete and iron. The work cost some 288 million pesetas and involved the Club's indebtedness for a few years. Although initially it did not have a name, a survey among the members held in September 1965 established the CF Barcelona Stadium as the official name. In any case, since 1957 it was popularly known as Camp Nou, since the stadium replaced the old Les Corts field, the old field. In April 2001 the name of Camp Nou was made official after a popular consultation, through mail, among the members of the Entity. Of the 29,102 responses sent to the Club, a total of 19,861 (68.25%) opted for Camp Nou instead of the FC Barcelona Stadium.

The Stadium has a maximum height of 49.67 meters and an area of ​​58,760 square meters (260 meters long and 226 wide). Following UEFA regulations, the playing field is 105 mx 68 m. With a capacity of 99,354 people, it is currently the largest stadium in all of Europe. The capacity of the Stadium, however, has undergone several changes, due to the different extensions that have been made. Initially, in 1957 there was room for 99,053 spectators, a figure that reached 115,000 in 1982, coinciding with the celebration of the World Cup. Subsequently, in application of the regulations that forced the elimination of standing seats, the capacity was once again, at the end of the 90s, in the line of 99,000 spectators. During the 1998/99 season, UEFA recognized the services and functionality of the Camp Nou and classified it as a Five Star stadium. In 2010, with the new UEFA regulations, this category was replaced by Category 4, which corresponds to stadiums that meet the highest requirements in terms of performance, services and capacity, as is the case of the FC Barcelona Stadium. Among all the facilities and rooms inside the Stadium, a small chapel stands out next to the changing rooms, the boxes, the VIP room, the press room, several television sets, the press booths, the technical services offices , the Sports Medicine Center, the Operational Control Unit (UCO), the premises of the Barça Players Association, the FC Barcelona Museum and offices of various Club departments.
